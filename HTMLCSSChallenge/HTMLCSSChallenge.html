In Loving Memory of Mike-Winston

Romani
Mike-Winston

On November 6, 2019 the Epling family said goodbye to a beloved member of the family, Mike-Winston. He was estimated to be about 20 years of age, and due to either back or pastern problems, deemed unrideable. This did not stop him from walking on many adventures, the few short years he was with the Epling family. He loved to see new places, eat new grasses. He was an excellent companion to Romani and beloved by all who met him and a list of surviving relatives is below.

Equine Comparisons

Mike-Winston

Mike-Winston arrived to the Epling family some 4 or 5 years ago, when a previous horse had passed away and a companion was needed for the current horse, Windy. Mike-Winston was about the same size and coloring of the horse that passed away, and he was in need of some tender loving care, he had been abandoned at the barn, several hundred pounds underweight and hooves severely neglected. It was determined that an unknown injury meant that riding was not possible, but Mike-Winston could still hike! So hike we did! All over the hills of Gaston, Oregon. His pain being too much to even hike comfortable, we made the humane decision to put him down November 6, 2019.

Romani

Romani, an Arabian gelding, arrived to the Epling family February of 2017. His previous owners were seeking to get out of equine ownership and wanted him to go to a family that would appreciate him. He was deemed lively and requires some more training into the human-equine relationship prior to regular riding of any sort. He enjoys the company of other horses very much, and since the passing of Mike-Winston, is enjoying the company of his pasture neighbor, Fynn.
